What was the Y2K bug?

Every computer had something known as an “internal clock,” which dictated all time based functions. However, there was an issue. The clocks were only designed to go up to 1999. As the new millennium approached, computer scientists came to realize that once the date happened, all internal clocks would reset to 1900, disrupting nearly all interconnected functions. We were helpless as the date rolled around, and held our breaths for the collapse…
